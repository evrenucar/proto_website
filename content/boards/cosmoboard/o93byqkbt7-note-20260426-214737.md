zaaa### 1. Who is Eligible?
Apple’s education pricing isn't just for college students. The discount applies to:
a* **Current and newly accepted university/college students.**
* **Parents** buying for a university/college student.
aaaaaaaa* **Teachers, faculty, and staff** at all levels (from kindergarten to higher education).
* **Homeschool teachers.**

### 2. How Much Can You Save?
The discount varies depending on the device, but you can generally expect:
* **Macs:** Roughly $100 to $200 (or €100 to €200) off the standard price for MacBooks (Air and Pro), iMacs, Mac minis, and desktop displays.
* **AppleCare+:** You also get a discount—usually around 10%—on AppleCare+ for your Mac.
* **Software:** Apple offers a "Pro Apps Bundle for Education" which includes Final Cut Pro, Logic Pro, Motion, Compressor, and MainStage for a steeply discounted flat price.

*(Note: Apple rarely discounts iPhones or Apple Watches for education.)*

### 3. How to Claim the Discount
* **Online:** You can buy directly through the **Apple Education Store** website. Depending on your region (like in the UK or the Netherlands), Apple uses a service called **UNiDAYS** to verify your student or staff status before letting you enter the store. In other regions (like the US), it operates on a bit of an honor system online, but Apple can randomly audit your purchase and ask for a student ID.
* **In-Store:** Simply walk into any physical Apple Store and show a valid student ID, college acceptance letter, or faculty/staff badge at checkout. 

### 4. Pro-Tip: Wait for "Back to School" Promos (if you can)
While the education discount is available 365 days a year, Apple runs a special "Back to School" promotion every summer (usually mid-June to late September in the Northern Hemisphere, or January to March in the Southern Hemisphere). 

During this window, you get the standard education discount **plus** a free bonus—usually a gift card ranging from $100 to $150 (or a free pair of AirPods/Apple Pencil, depending on the year's promotion) with the purchase of a qualifying Mac or iPad.
